Reflection Summary

Two educations at once — one in software, one in the world.

Spending six weeks in Cape Town as a Software Engineering Intern pushed me well beyond the scope of a typical internship, both professionally and personally. Contributing to MERLS for GRIT taught me how much design and communication matter as much as technical skill when building for a client with a real mission — one centered on gender rights that added weight to every decision we made about the platform.

But just as formative were the experiences outside the office. Touring Khayelitsha township was the single most eye-opening moment of the trip — seeing the wealth gap in Cape Town up close, and still finding a community in great spirits, playing soccer in the street and singing with kids at an informal elementary school, reframed how I think about resilience. Learning about apartheid at the Slave Lodge, hearing from Lionel Davis, and visiting the District Six Museum showed me how recent and how deeply race-based this history is — much closer to the present than I'd realized. Talking with high schoolers at Curro Delft about the digital divide in their community connected directly back to why the work I was doing mattered. And then there were the moments I'll just never forget for their own sake: the view from Table Mountain in every direction, penguins at Boulders Beach, lions and elephants on safari.

I'm coming back with a clearer sense of how I want to use my CS degree — building things that solve real problems for people who need them — and a much bigger picture of the world than I had six weeks ago.

Week by Week

Lessons gained, route by route.

Each week paired hands-on work on MERLS with an excursion or cultural experience — the two threads that shaped the trip together.

Week
01

Orientation, Table Mountain, and Apartheid History

Our first week combined project orientation with an intense introduction to South Africa's history. We met with GRIT's Ronel to understand their needs and built our initial project pitch, while outside of work I hiked Table Mountain — the views in every direction, mountains, ocean, and city at once, were unlike anything I'd seen before. A lecture on apartheid and a visit to the Slave Lodge were the bigger gut-check of the week: I hadn't realized how recent this history was, or how entirely it was built on race. Understanding how enslaved people built so much of the city added weight to everything else I saw that week.

[ Slave Lodge / team pitch ]
Week
02

New Direction, and a Township That Changed My Perspective

This week we dove into new documentation from GRIT and began building, using Claude Code to stand up an initial frontend, backend, and database. The bigger lesson of the week, though, came from touring the Khayelitsha township. Seeing families living in informal settlements they'd built themselves, in stark contrast to the wealth elsewhere in Cape Town, was the most eye-opening experience of the trip. What struck me most was that despite the conditions, everyone was in great spirits — we played soccer in the street and sang with kids at an informal elementary school. A lecture from Lionel Davis and a visit to the District Six Museum added even more depth to understanding the city's history.

[ District Six Museum ]
Week
03

Building Independently, Cape Malay Food, and a High School Showcase

Working fully remotely, I helped build out core pages of MERLS and put together our software architecture presentation, learning to communicate and self-organize as a team without a client meeting that week. Outside of work, a Cape Malay cooking class introduced me to an incredible chicken curry, and visiting Curro Delft High School for a tech-for-community-problems showcase gave me new insight into challenges like the digital divide directly from the students living it.

[ Cape Malay cooking class ]
[ Curro Delft showcase ]
Week
04

Designing for the User, and San Heritage

I built new features for MERLS, including a Gantt-style timeline and calendar page, and attended a data collection workshop with GRIT that reinforced the importance of designing around the people actually using the product. That same lesson echoed at the !Khwa ttu San Heritage Centre, learning how South Africa's indigenous San people were displaced by colonization. Cape Point and Chapman's Peak brought a completely different kind of unforgettable — Elands, springbok, and ostriches along the drive, and penguins at Boulders Beach.

[ Boulders Beach penguins ]
[ Cape Point / Chapman's Peak ]
Week
05

AI Features, Community Impact, and Safari

Alongside implementing AI-powered daily insights, Google Analytics integration, and CSV uploads, I attended a workshop on technology-facilitated gender-based violence and a usability testing session — both reminders of the real impact GRIT has on people's lives. A day safari topped off the week: seeing lions, rhinos, giraffes, elephants, and hippos living wild was a surreal experience I won't forget.

[ Safari wildlife ]
[ GRIT workshop ]
Week
06

Final Deployment, and Saying Goodbye to Cape Town

In our final week, I incorporated usability feedback into the platform, helped deploy MERLS to Railway, and delivered our final presentation to GRIT. We closed out the trip with traditional djembe drumming and a dinner of dishes from across Africa — a fitting send-off after six weeks that changed how I think about both technology and the world.
